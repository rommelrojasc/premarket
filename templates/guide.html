How to read each section, what the terms mean, and when to use the dashboard throughout the trading day.

Macro Event Warning

What you see
!! MACRO EVENT: PPI -- pre-market signals may be less reliable

Appears only on days with scheduled macro events: CPI, PPI, FOMC, NFP, GDP, PCE. These events release at specific times (usually 8:30 AM or 2:00 PM ET) and can reverse the entire pre-market trend in seconds.

When you see this
Treat all model predictions with extra skepticism. Pre-market price action before 8:30 AM on CPI day tells you almost nothing about post-release direction. Wait for the event to drop, let the market digest it (~15 min), then refresh the dashboard.

Three cards, one per time horizon. Each predicts the direction of SPY relative to the 9:30 AM open.

Confidence % The averaged probability from three ML models (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, Logistic Regression). Not a guarantee -- a 70% confidence means the ensemble gives ~70% probability to that direction.
NO EDGE Shown when confidence is below 60%. The model doesn't have a strong enough signal. This is intentional -- it prevents you from anchoring on a weak, coin-flip prediction. No trade is the default.
LOW CONF Confidence between 60-75%. The model sees something, but it's not strong. Size down, or use only as a lean/tie-breaker alongside other analysis.
HIGH CONF Confidence above 75%. The three models meaningfully agree. These are the signals most worth acting on. Check the scorecard to see how high-conf signals have performed historically.
P(bull) Raw probability the model assigns to a bullish outcome. Shown at the bottom of each card. P(bull) = 0.65 means 65% chance of up, 35% chance of down.
How to use
The 30-minute card is most actionable for scalps and quick entries. The full-day card tells you the broader bias -- whether to hold winners or cut early. If all three agree, the signal is strongest. If they disagree (e.g., bear 30m but bull day), expect early weakness that reverses.

Overnight Gap % change from yesterday's RTH close to today's pre-market open (4:00 AM). Measures how overnight futures/news moved the market. A big gap up/down often gets "filled" (reversed) during the session.
Pre-market Move % change within the pre-market window (4:00 - 9:30 AM). Shows if buyers or sellers are in control this morning. This is what the ML model primarily reads.
Yesterday RTH Yesterday's regular trading hours return (open to close). Context for mean-reversion: a big down day often sees a bounce, and vice versa.

IBS (Internal Bar Strength)

Formula: (Close - Low) / (High - Low) for yesterday's candle. Ranges from 0 to 1.

IBS < 0.2 (oversold) Yesterday closed near its low. Historically bullish for the next day -- mean-reversion buyers tend to step in.
IBS > 0.8 (overbought) Yesterday closed near its high. Historically bearish for the next day -- sellers tend to take profits.
IBS 0.2 - 0.8 (neutral) No strong mean-reversion signal from yesterday's candle.

VIX (Volatility Index)

The "fear gauge." Measures expected S&P 500 volatility over the next 30 days.

VIX < 18 LOW Calm markets. Moves are smaller, trends are steady, premium selling works well. Breakout traps are more common.
VIX 18-25 ELEVATED Choppy. Bigger intraday swings, wider stops needed. Both trend and mean-reversion can work but sizing should be reduced.
VIX > 25 HIGH Fear in the market. Large, fast moves in both directions. Size down significantly. Great for long premium (straddles). Terrible for selling premium or tight stops.

Shows "--" before pre-market opens or if IBKR can't provide the data.

Options Walls & GEX

This section uses real-time options chain data to identify key price levels and the current gamma regime. It tells you where support/resistance lives and how the market will behave when price moves.

Put Wall & Call Wall

What you see
PUT WALL (SUPPORT)
560
OI: 45K -- MMs buy here, floor
CALL WALL (RESIST)
580
OI: 38K -- MMs sell here, ceiling

Put Wall = strike with the most put open interest below current price. Market makers who sold those puts need to buy stock as price approaches this level (delta hedging). This creates a natural floor / support.

Call Wall = strike with the most call open interest above current price. Market makers sell stock here to hedge. Natural ceiling / resistance.

How to use
If SPY is at 570, put wall is 560, and call wall is 580: expect SPY to bounce between these levels. Price will struggle to break above 580 (MM selling pressure) and will find buyers near 560 (MM hedging). Use these as profit targets and stop-loss levels.

Max Pain

The strike price where the most options expire worthless -- where option holders collectively lose the most money. Market makers profit the most at this level.

Price tends to gravitate toward max pain into expiration, especially on Fridays. This is sometimes called "pinning." The effect is strongest in the last 2 hours of trading on expiration day.

GEX Flip

The price level where net gamma exposure crosses from positive to negative. This is the regime boundary -- the market behaves fundamentally differently on each side.

Gamma Exposure (GEX) & Gamma Zones

Gamma Exposure measures how much market makers need to hedge as price moves. It determines whether the market dampens or amplifies moves.

Positive Gamma (+γ)

SPY is ABOVE the GEX Flip level. Dealers are LONG gamma. When price moves up, they sell. When it moves down, they buy. Their hedging dampens every move.

Expect: Range-bound, mean-reverting, choppy action
Trade: Fade breakouts, buy dips, sell premium (iron condors)
Avoid: Chasing breakouts, trend-following
Negative Gamma (-γ)

SPY is BELOW the GEX Flip level. Dealers are SHORT gamma. When price moves up, they must buy more. When it moves down, they sell more. Their hedging amplifies every move.

Expect: Trending, volatile, outsized moves in both directions
Trade: Ride trends, breakouts are real, buy directional
Avoid: Mean-reversion, fading moves, tight stops, selling premium

P/C Ratio (Put/Call Ratio)

P/C < 0.8 (bullish skew) More call open interest than puts. Market positioning is optimistic.
P/C 0.8-1.2 (neutral) Balanced positioning. No strong directional skew.
P/C > 1.2 (bearish skew) More puts than calls. Market positioning is defensive / fearful. Can also be a contrarian bullish signal if extreme.

Daily Sequence

When and how to use the dashboard throughout the trading day.

~4:00 - 6:00 AM ET
First Look (optional)
Pre-market opens at 4:00 AM. Volume is very thin. Predictions will mostly use proxy features from yesterday's data since there isn't enough pre-market activity yet. Useful to check for overnight gaps and macro event warnings, but don't trade on the predictions alone.
~7:00 - 8:00 AM ET
Primary Read
This is the sweet spot. Enough pre-market volume for the model to extract real features: VWAP deviation, volume acceleration, momentum slope, RSI. Refresh the dashboard now. This is when you form your directional bias for the day.
  • Check prediction cards -- do you have an edge?
  • Read market context -- gap direction, IBS, VIX
  • Note macro warning if present
~8:30 AM ET
Macro Event Drop
CPI, PPI, NFP, and other major data releases drop at 8:30 AM. If you see the orange macro warning, wait for the event. The market will move sharply -- pre-market predictions made before this are unreliable. Wait for the dust to settle (~8:45 AM), then refresh.
~9:00 - 9:25 AM ET
Final Read
Refresh one last time for the fullest pre-market picture. This is your final read before the opening bell.
  • Re-check prediction cards with latest data
  • Compare to previous predictions -- did signal strengthen or weaken?
  • Review options/GEX section -- walls, gamma zone, scenarios
  • Identify which trading scenario matches current conditions
  • Set your plan: entry, target, stop
9:30 AM ET
Market Open -- Execute
Execute based on your read. Use the trading scenarios table to guide real-time decisions. The first 5 minutes are chaotic -- most traders wait until 9:35-9:40 for the opening range to form.
  • Watch for the scenario that's playing out
  • Use put wall / call wall as profit targets
  • Respect the gamma zone -- trend-follow in -γ, mean-revert in +γ
  • If no edge was shown, stay flat or scalp only
2:00 - 4:00 PM ET
Into Close
Max pain gravity is strongest in the last 2 hours, especially on Fridays (0DTE expiration). Price tends to drift toward max pain. Check the scenarios table for "Into close" guidance.
After Close
Scorecard Update
The next time you load the dashboard, the scorecard auto-updates with actual outcomes for today. Review whether the model was right or wrong, and track how high-confidence signals perform over time. This builds your trust calibration.

Reading Signals Together

The dashboard sections aren't meant to be read in isolation. Here's how they combine.

Strong Bullish Setup

Example
Prediction cards: BULL across all horizons, 30M at 72%, Day at 78% HIGH CONF
Context: Overnight gap +0.3%, PM move +0.1%, IBS was 0.15 (oversold), VIX LOW
Options: Positive gamma, price near put wall (support), max pain above current price

Read: Everything lines up. Model, context, and options all say up. Positive gamma means moves are dampened, so a steady grind higher is likely. Put wall provides a floor. Size up.

Conflicting Signals

Example
Prediction cards: 30M BEAR 65%, Morning NO EDGE, Day BULL 62%
Context: Gap up +0.5%, PM move -0.2% (fading the gap), IBS 0.85 (overbought)
Options: Negative gamma, near call wall

Read: Model says weak open but recovery by EOD. Pre-market is fading a big gap. IBS is overbought. Negative gamma means the fade could accelerate. Wait. Let the 30-minute card play out, then reassess for the day trade. Don't force it.

No Edge Day

Example
Prediction cards: NO EDGE across all three horizons
Context: Gap 0.0%, PM move flat, IBS neutral, VIX ELEVATED
Options: Positive gamma, near max pain

Read: The model has nothing. Context is flat. Positive gamma + near max pain = expect pinning and chop. Stay flat or sell premium. This is the dashboard doing its job -- keeping you out of a coin-flip trade.

When to Ignore the Dashboard

Macro event days CPI, FOMC rate decisions, and NFP can move SPY 1-2% in minutes. Pre-market signals are meaningless before the release. Wait for the dust to settle.
Before 7 AM Pre-market volume is too thin for reliable feature extraction. The model is mostly guessing from yesterday's data.
Black swan events Geopolitical events, surprise Fed actions, market circuit breakers. The model was trained on normal distributions. Tail events break all models.
Scorecard accuracy below 50% If high-conf predictions have been wrong more than right over the last 15 days, the model is miscalibrated. Force retrain with python run.py --train or stop using the signals until accuracy recovers.
Options data unavailable If the options section shows "unavailable," you're missing half the picture. The prediction cards still work, but without GEX/walls you can't identify key price levels or gamma regime. Trade smaller without this context.
